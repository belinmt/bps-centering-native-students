Description: Make observations and measurements to identify materials based on their properties.

In-class exercise: Materials needed: scale or balance, jars with lids, thermometer, marker, soil collected from different natural local locations.

Fill a jar halfway full with water and close tightly with a lid. Stick a thermometer in the water and weigh the jar. Record the weight and the temperature. Then, stick the jar outside to be warmed by the sun. After several minutes, record the temperature and weight.

Next, use the jar and water to conduct soil texture activity (https://www.soils4kids.org/files/s4k/soil-texture-experiment.pdf). Tell students where soils were collected from so they can figure out the soil texture in those locations.

Refer to the “Background Agricultural Connections” portion of this website (https://agclassroom.org/matrix/lesson/print/365/) to talk to students about the importance of knowing soil texture for farming. Next, mix two different types of soil samples and redo the soil texture analysis.
