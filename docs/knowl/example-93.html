Example 2.3.85. Standard MS-ETS1-3.

Description: Analyze data from tests to determine similarities and differences among several design solutions to identify the best characteristics of each that can be combined into a new solution to better meet the criteria for success.

In-class exercise: Introduce students to the Rio Grande Silvery Minnow (https://www.wildlife.state.nm.us/download/education/conservation/wildlife-notes/aquatic/Rio-Grande-silvery-minnow.pdf).

Ask students to research and evaluate different designs and strategies for helping the Rio Grande silvery minnow population recover and grow.

Discuss with students about the impacts of climate change in the Rio Grande (https://nmindepth.com/2016/08/09/rio-grande-river-101/) on silvery minnow recovery and discuss ideas on how recovery strategies might be adapted to address climate change.

Take home exercise: Ask students to ask family or community member what the word is for the silvery minnow in their Native language and how their Pueblo interacts or used to interact with the silvery minnow.
