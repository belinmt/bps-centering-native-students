Section 4.2 Resources

Subsection 4.2.1 Indigenous Studies

  • International Indian Treaty Council Program Area on Climate Change 35  This is an Indigenous based organization that is advocating for immediate education and action on climate change worldwide.

  • Culturally Responsive Curriculum Framework 36  This is the framework created by BPS Indian Education to help guide teachers in their approaches toward implementing culturally responsive curriculum.

  • Indigenous Governance Database 37  TThis database is available through the Native Nations Institute (NNI) at the University of Arizona. Dr. Michelle Suina 38  of Cochiti Pueblo, is recognized by NNI for her work in relation to health and Indigenous data sovereignty.

  • United States Indigenous Data Sovereignty Network 39  According to the homepage: “The United States Indigenous Data Sovereignty Network (USIDSN) helps ensure that data for and about Indigenous nations and peoples in the US (American Indians, Alaska Natives, and Native Hawaiians) are utilized to advance Indigenous aspirations for collective and individual wellbeing. USIDSN’s primary function is to provide research information and policy advocacy to safeguard the rights and promote the interests of Indigenous nations and peoples in relation to data.”

Subsection 4.2.2 Science

  • Teachers’ Guide to Valles Caldera 40 . This site includes a climate change component.

  • Article about how El Nino and La Nina influence climate change and how the peoples living in Chaco Canyon may have been affected by drought caused by La Nina https://www.nature.com/scitable/knowledge/library/el-nino-s-grip-on-climate-25816069/

  • Report by the Institute for Tribal Environmental Professionals on climate change effortsby tribes in NM and AZ https://www7.nau.edu/itep/main/tcc/docs/resources/SWTCCEffortsAZNM_12-14-11.pdf

  • A video talking about the NM Tribal Resiliency Action Network (NM TRAN) and their work to address climate change

  • Fires and Climate Change presentation by Maurice Cruz

  • South Central Climate Adaptation Science Center- Native Nations Youth Resources https://southcentralclimate.org/native-nations/youth-resources/

  • Run to Be Visible 41 . A short documentary about Dr. Lydia Jennings pursuit to honor Indigenous scientists of the past, present, and future.

Subsection 4.2.3 Mathematics

  • Show Me Your Math 42 A program that invites First Nations students to explore mathematics that is evident in their own community and cultural practices.

  • Traditional Indigenous Mathematics in the Ontario Classroom 43  Educator Bryan Bellefuille describes how he incorporates traditional Indigenous knowledge in to mathematics.

  • Radical Math 44  A collection of curriculum, lesson planning, and data sets that can be searched and adapted by math topic and social justice issues.

  • Learning for Justice 45  Another collection of curriculum, lesson planning, and data sets that include mathematics lessons, but is not restricted to mathematics.

  • Teacher’s Climate Guide 46 A collection of activities and exercises connecting climate change and high school level mathematics

  • IndigeSTEAM 47 An organization promoting Indigneous-led and culturally relevant programming in STEAM.

  • INDIGIDATA 48 An Indigenous-faculty led workshop introducing data science and informatics skills to tribal undergraduate and graduate students.

  • DATA.GOV 49 A repository of data, tools, and resources that can be used for research and exploration. This is can be searched to find data collected from local communities.
